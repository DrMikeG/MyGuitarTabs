I love tabs, gives you the clues when training your ear. I however noticed among some of the other tabs an easier way but sometimes not a full feel.

The tuning on this song is dropped half a step to Eflat. (if you want to play with the song)

For the accoustic part, I'm using the lower strings to give the sound a deeper feel. 

I am also showing you this with a dropped D for added depth. (that is the sixth string dropped down a whole step)

On the Second set of chords, you can get away with muffling (or not striking) the 5th and 6th strings to make life easier since the majority of the melody we hear on the guitar is being dictated by the 2nd and 3rd strings.

Drift and Die -- Puddle of Mudd:  Db tuning

NOTE:  This song is certainly not as easy as the tabs on this site lead
       one to believe...especially for the lone acoustic guitarist.  So,
       here are some "tips."  On the intro riff, play the Dm-2 chord 
       with your first and your third fingers.  Next, add your second
       finger to form the D2 chord.  Now, you're probably struggling 
       between "dugh" and "no dugh."  Please wait.  To form the Dm2/C
       chord, slide your first finger "up" to the 6th fret on the second
       string and hit the 8th fret of the low "E" with your pinky.  Leave
       your second finger dangling, and "drape" your pinky over the 5th string
       to mute it while strumming all strings except the high "e."  Your
       pinky and the "open D fourth string" form the power on this chord
       and should ring loud and clear.  Now, to form the Dm2/B chord, 
       use that dangling second finger to hit the 7th fret of the low "E"
       while removing the pinky.  

       --rock on, kidguitar
